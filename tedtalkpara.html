Have you ever stopped to wonder what truly drives innovation? It’s not just intelligence, or resources, or even timing—it’s curiosity.
That deep, relentless urge to ask why, and then ask what if.
Some of the greatest breakthroughs in history began with a simple question.
The lightbulb wasn’t invented by someone trying to make a bulb—it was made by someone trying to light up the world.
And in that same spirit, every one of us holds the power to spark change.
Not by being perfect, but by being curious, by being bold enough to imagine a different future—and then brave enough to build it.
